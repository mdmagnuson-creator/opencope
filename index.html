Why You Cope

The cope makes sense. The fear is justified. But denial doesn't change the trajectory - it just leaves you less prepared for it.

01
AI Fatigue

Years of hype. Self-driving cars that never drove themselves. Chatbots that couldn't chat. Blockchain solving everything. We're tired of being told everything will change. So we dismiss this too.

02
It's Real

Deep down, there's a suspicion this time is different. And that's terrifying. Easier to dismiss it than to sit with the implications. If we don't look directly at it, maybe it won't look at us.

03
Livelihoods

Developers. Designers. Product managers. Support staff. Entire careers built on skills that might become... ambient. The cope isn't irrational - it's self-preservation.

But the cope isn't helping. Let's look at what you're coping with.

The Window

Here's the uncomfortable truth the memo lays out:

"The build vs. buy decision just collapsed to zero."

Software creation as we know it is dissolving. Not because AI is bad at coding - because it's getting good at it. Good enough that the constraint isn't "knowing how to build" anymore. It's "knowing what to build."

"No UI you design is more efficient than your personal agent just answering on your behalf."

Your beautifully crafted dashboard? Your elegant form flows? Your carefully considered UX decisions? They're bridges across a gap that's closing. When the machine has enough context to act on your behalf, it doesn't need your interface to collect information from you.

There is a window. It's open now. The destination is clear, but the path still requires navigation.

The window will close. Context will become ambient. Software will dissolve.

But today is not that day, and there is still building to be done.

So Now What?

This isn't a doom site. We're not here to tell you everything is hopeless.

The window is open. There's meaningful work to be done while context remains the constraint. Systems can be built, problems solved, value created - all within a paradigm that has an expiration date but hasn't expired yet.

The question isn't whether things will change. They will.

The question is: what are you going to do while the window is still open?
